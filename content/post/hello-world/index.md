I struggled to tighten the clasp on the back of my cap. My head had not shrunk since my last wear, but the lump of my bun tucked into my hijab no longer proved a barrier. I would only be enjoying the weather in the sanctity of my enclosed backyard, so modesty no longer applied. 

I did a double take in the tinted mirror on my way down the stairs. I hadn't meant to match my cotton candy top to my cap. It was just the first one that came out of my drawer. With my glossy lips colored the same shade and my ponytail swinging out the back with an unfamiliar rhythm, I was stunning. I wasn't used to being stunning. 

A girl reading in the sun shouldn’t have been so stunning, but this was a different girl. The girl who only exists between two wooden fences, a brick wall, and a forest that backs onto train tracks. The girl only seen on days sunny enough to warrant UV protection and windy enough that the pages of her book won't overly flutter. I was pleased to meet her. I was pleased to be her.
